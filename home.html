 WHAT IS GREEN TECHNOLOGY

The escalating global environmental crisis underscores the critical importance of green technology and innovation. This section not only delineates the magnitude of the challenges we face, including climate change, resource depletion, and pollution but also underscores the pivotal role technology plays in providing sustainable solutions.

Environment technology or green technology is also known as clean technology, is the application of one or more of environmental science, green chemistry, environmental monitoring systems which includes the examples like plant based packaging, green architecture used in everyday works through electronic devices which can be used to improve the quality of nature as save it . Green technology can be implemented by innovation in which we innovate new devices through which the quality of nature and our mother earth can be saved. 

 How it is linked to Innovation ?

It includes everything from cutting-edge waste management techniques, water purification, and even environmentally friendly transportation choices, to renewable energy sources like wind and solar power.

Green technology innovation, as opposed to traditional technological innovation modes, is more focused on low carbon development and provides vital help to nations across the globe in their fight against climate change. By modernizing the industrial structure, the impact of new developments in green technology on carbon emissions is taken into account using fixed effect and mediating effect models.



How does vertical farming contribute to green technology?


Vertical farming is a green technology that allows for the cultivation of crops in vertically stacked layers or structures. It reduces the land required for farming, conserves water usage, and minimizes the need for pesticides or herbicides.



How can green technology be applied in waste management?


Green technology can be applied in waste management through the implementation of recycling programs, the use of composting techniques, the development of waste-to-energy technologies, and the promotion of sustainable consumption practices.
